Calculator Accuracy & Limitations

Our calculators produce mathematical estimates based on the inputs you provide and the formulas we implement. Results may differ from actual loan terms, payments, or interest charges for several reasons:

  • Lender-specific fees: Origination fees, processing charges, insurance premiums, and other lender-specific costs are not included in basic EMI calculations unless explicitly entered.
  • Variable rate changes: Variable and adjustable-rate loan calculations assume a constant rate. Actual payments will change when benchmark rates adjust.
  • Rounding and compounding conventions: Different lenders use different compounding periods, payment timing conventions, and rounding methods. Small differences can accumulate over long loan tenures.
  • Tax implications: Rate tables and interest cost comparisons do not account for the tax deductibility of mortgage interest or student loan interest in your jurisdiction.
  • Input accuracy: Results are only as accurate as the inputs you provide. Always verify your loan's actual interest rate, origination date, and remaining balance before using these figures for planning.

Interest Rate Information

Rate ranges shown on DoraTools (such as "Mortgage: 6.5–7.5%") are general market estimates intended to illustrate typical ranges. They are not quotes, offers, or guarantees from any lender. Your actual rate will depend on your credit score, loan-to-value ratio, debt-to-income ratio, the specific lender, market conditions at the time of your application, and many other factors.

Rate information is updated periodically but may not reflect real-time market conditions. Always obtain official rate quotes directly from qualified lenders before making any borrowing decisions.
