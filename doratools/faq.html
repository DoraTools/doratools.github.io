An EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is a fixed monthly payment made to repay a loan. Each payment covers both interest and principal. Early payments are weighted heavily toward interest; later payments shift toward principal — this is called amortization.
EMI = [P × R × (1+R)^N] ÷ [(1+R)^N − 1]. P = Principal, R = Monthly rate (annual ÷ 12 ÷ 100), N = Number of monthly payments. Example: $10,000 at 6% for 3 years → EMI = $304.22/month, total interest = $1,152.
Longer tenure lowers your monthly payment but significantly increases total interest. A $20,000 loan at 8%: 3-year total interest = $2,562. 7-year total interest = $6,139 — $3,577 more for the same loan. Always calculate total cost, not just monthly payment.
Most lenders consider under 36% excellent, 36–43% acceptable, and above 43% high-risk. For personal financial health, keep all monthly debt payments below 30% of take-home pay — leaving meaningful room for savings and unexpected expenses.
Flat rate charges interest on the original principal for the entire tenure. Reducing balance charges interest only on the outstanding declining balance each month.
