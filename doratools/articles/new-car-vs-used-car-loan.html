New Car vs Used Car Loan 2026 — Which Is Cheaper Over 5 Years?


New car: $38,000 at 6.5% for 5 years. Used car (3 years old): $22,000 at 10% for 4 years. The used car monthly payment is lower — but which is actually cheaper over 5 years of total ownership?

Total 5-Year Cost: New Car

Loan total payments: $44,500. Insurance (5 yr): $8,500. Maintenance: $3,000. Depreciation loss: $15,200 (40% of value). 5-year total: $56,000. Remaining market value: $22,800. Net cost: $33,200.

Total 5-Year Cost: Used Car

Loan total payments: $25,600. Insurance (5 yr): $5,200. Maintenance: $5,500 (higher for older vehicle). Depreciation: $7,700 (35% of value). 5-year total: $44,000. Remaining value: $14,300. Net cost: $29,700.

🚗 Result: Used car saves $3,500 over 5 years despite the higher interest rate. The higher rate on used car loans is dwarfed by the lower purchase price and dramatically slower depreciation curve.

When New Car Wins

  • Manufacturer 0% or deeply subsidized financing offer
  • You keep the vehicle 8–12 years
  • Reliability is non-negotiable for professional reasons
  • Used car market in your area lacks trustworthy inspection options
