How to Raise Your Credit Score 100 Points in 6 Months


A 100-point credit score improvement can reduce your mortgage rate by 0.5–1.5%, saving $40,000–$120,000 on a 30-year mortgage. Here is the exact 6-month execution plan.

Month 1–2: Stop the Bleeding

  • Set up autopay for every single account — late payments are the number-one score killer
  • Stop applying for any new credit — each hard inquiry costs 5–10 points
  • Pull your free credit report and check for errors at annualcreditreport.com

Month 3–4: Attack Utilization

Credit utilization (balance ÷ credit limit) drives 30% of your score. Getting each card below 30% utilization has an immediate, measurable impact. Getting below 10% is optimal and can add 20–40 points alone.

Month 5–6: Dispute and Optimize

  • Dispute any incorrect negative items in writing — bureaus must investigate within 30 days
  • Request credit limit increases on existing cards (improves utilization ratio without spending more)
  • Become an authorized user on a family member's old, high-limit, clean-history account

📈 Following this plan consistently moves most scores 60–100 points in 6 months. The biggest single-action gains: paying down credit card balances and clearing incorrect negative items from your report.
