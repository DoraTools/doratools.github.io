First Personal Loan? 10 Costly Mistakes to Avoid Before You Sign


The loan agreement contains clauses that can cost you hundreds or thousands of dollars. Most first-time borrowers skip straight to the signature line. Read these 10 warnings first.

1. Comparing Interest Rate Instead of APR

APR (Annual Percentage Rate) includes origination fees and gives the true cost comparison. A 12% rate with 3% origination may have a higher APR than a 13% rate with zero origination on short-tenure loans.

2. Ignoring the Origination Fee

Typically 1–8% of loan amount, deducted before you receive funds. A $10,000 loan with 5% origination means you receive $9,500 but repay $10,000 + interest.

3. Not Checking Prepayment Penalty

Some lenders charge 1–5% of outstanding balance for early payoff. This eliminates the entire benefit of prepaying. It must be disclosed — read the clause before signing.

4. Choosing Longest Tenure for Lowest Payment

$15,000 at 14% for 3 years: total interest = $3,468. Same loan for 5 years: total interest = $5,940. The more affordable option costs $2,472 more. Always calculate total cost, not just monthly payment.

5. Accepting Bundled Payment Protection Insurance

Adds $30–$100/month and is presented as mandatory but is almost always optional. Decline it. Get separate term life coverage if you genuinely need protection.

Mistakes 6–10

  • Not reading the late payment clause — fees and grace period vary widely
  • Borrowing more than needed — every extra dollar costs interest
  • Not getting multiple quotes — rates vary 3–8% across lenders for same profile
  • Missing the auto-payment discount — many lenders offer 0.25–0.5% rate reduction
  • Applying to multiple lenders simultaneously — each is a hard inquiry dropping your score

✅ Golden rule: Calculate total cost (not just EMI). Compare APR across at least 3 lenders. Read the prepayment clause. Never borrow more than you can comfortably repay within 36 months.
