Student Loan Interest Tax Deduction 2026 — Full Eligibility Guide


Up to $2,500 in student loan interest is deductible as an above-the-line deduction — meaning you do not need to itemize to claim it. This is genuinely free money that millions of borrowers miss every year.

Income Phase-Out Range

The deduction begins phasing out at $75,000 MAGI (single filers) and $155,000 (married filing jointly). Above $90,000 single or $185,000 joint: no deduction at all. Within the range: partial deduction calculated proportionally.

How Much Can You Actually Save?

Maximum deduction: $2,500. At 22% tax bracket: saves $550/year. At 24%: saves $600/year. Small but completely free — you are legally owed this money, so claim it.

Who Qualifies

  • Individual who took the loan for their own higher education, or for a spouse or dependent
  • Loan must be from a qualified financial institution — not family members
  • Course must be post-secondary education
  • Both federal and private student loans qualify if they meet the criteria

📚 Income-Driven Repayment Trap: On IDR plans, your monthly payment may be lower than the monthly interest accruing on your balance. This creates negative amortization — you pay faithfully every month and your balance grows. If pursuing loan forgiveness, this can be intentional strategy. Otherwise, consider paying extra to prevent balance growth.
