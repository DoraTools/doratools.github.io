15-Year vs 30-Year Mortgage — The True Cost Difference in 2026


$300,000 mortgage. 30-year at 7% = $1,995/month, $418,000 total interest. 15-year at 6.5% = $2,613/month, $170,000 total interest. The 30-year costs $248,000 more. Yet millions of people choose it — and sometimes that is the right call.

The Mathematical Case for 15-Year

Pay $618/month more. Save $248,000 in interest. Build equity twice as fast. Own the home outright in half the time. If you can comfortably afford the higher payment, the 15-year is almost always the mathematically superior choice.

The Behavioral Case for 30-Year

The $618 monthly difference, invested in a broad market index fund averaging 10% annually over 15 years, grows to approximately $257,000 — slightly more than the $248,000 interest differential. The 30-year with disciplined investing mathematically breaks even.

📊 Honest assessment: If you will genuinely invest the monthly savings, the 30-year is competitive. If the extra $618 would be spent rather than invested — and for most people, it would — the 15-year wins decisively. Be honest about which describes you before choosing.

The Flexibility Argument

A 30-year mortgage with voluntary overpayments gives the best of both: low required payment in difficult months, aggressive payoff when cash flow allows. You can simulate a 15-year schedule by paying extra — but you cannot escape a 15-year commitment in a tight month.
